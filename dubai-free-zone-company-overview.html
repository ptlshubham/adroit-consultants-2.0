Steps to Form a Company in a Dubai Free Zone

1 .    Choose a Free Zone:

  • Identify the free zone that aligns with your business activity. Dubai has over 30 free zones, each catering to specific industries (e.g., Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC), Dubai Silicon Oasis (DSO), Jebel Ali Free Zone (JAFZA)).

    • 2 .    Select a Business Activity:

  • Choose the type of business activity you wish to undertake. Ensure the free zone allows this activity.

    • 3 .    Choose a Legal Structure:

  • Common legal structures in Dubai Free Zones:
  • Free Zone Establishment (FZE) – Single shareholder.
  • Free Zone Company (FZC) – Multiple shareholders.
  • Branch of a foreign/local company.

    • 4 .    Reserve a Company Name:

  • Choose and reserve a unique trade name for your business. Follow the guidelines of the free zone authority.

    • 5 .    Apply for Initial Approval:

  • Submit an application with basic details (business activity, shareholder details, etc.) to the free zone authority.

    • 6 .    Lease Office Space:

  • Secure an office or warehouse space in the chosen free zone as per the requirements of your license type.

    • 7 .    Submit Required Documents:

  • Common documentation includes:
  • Passport copies of shareholders and directors.
  • Visa copies (if applicable).
  • Proof of address.
  • Business plan (for specific activities).
  • Notarized Articles of Association (AOA) and Memorandum of Association (MOA).

    • 8 .    Obtain Licenses and Permits:

  • Get the required business license based on your activity, such as:
  • Trading license.
  • Service license.
  • Industrial license.

    • 9 .    Open a Bank Account:

  • Use your free zone company documents to open a corporate bank account.

    • 10 .    Apply for Visas:

  • Free zones offer investor, employee, and dependent visas based on the office size and employee quota.

    • Advantages of Setting Up a Company in a Dubai Free Zone

    • 100% Foreign Ownership: No requirement for a local Emirati partner or sponsor.
    • Tax Benefits: 0% corporate tax and personal income tax.
      Exemption from import/export duties.
    • Repatriation of Capital and Profits: 100% repatriation of profits and capital without restrictions.
    • Simplified Setup Process: ⦁ Streamlined registration process with fewer bureaucratic hurdles compared to mainland companies.
    • Sector-Specific Benefits: Free zones cater to specific industries, offering tailored facilities (e.g., tech parks, media hubs, logistics hubs).
    • World-Class Infrastructure: Access to modern office spaces, warehouses, and utilities designed for efficient operations.
    • Networking Opportunities: Presence of like-minded companies and industry peers fosters collaboration.
    • Visa Sponsorship: Free zones provide visa quotas for investors, employees, and dependents.
    • Flexible Office Options: Choose from virtual offices, shared workspaces, or full-fledged offices based on your budget and needs.
    • Ease of International Trade: Proximity to ports, airports, and logistics facilities ensures seamless import/export operations.
    • No Restriction on Currency: Conduct business in any currency with no foreign exchange restrictions.
    • Strategic Location: Dubai’s free zones are positioned strategically for global business connectivity.

    Popular Free Zones in Dubai

    1. DMCC (Dubai Multi Commodities Centre): Ideal for trading, commodities, and service businesses.
    2. JAFZA (Jebel Ali Free Zone): Best for logistics, manufacturing, and heavy industries.
    3. Dubai Internet City (DIC): Focused on IT and tech companies.
    4. Dubai Media City: Suitable for media and creative industries.
    5. Dubai Airport Free Zone (DAFZA): Ideal for aviation-related and trading businesses.

    By establishing a company in a Dubai free zone, businesses gain access to world-class infrastructure, significant cost advantages, and a business-friendly regulatory environment.
