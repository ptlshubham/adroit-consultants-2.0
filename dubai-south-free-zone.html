Drive your success forward at Dubai South Free Zone

Dubai South Free Zone (DSFZ) is a premier free zone located in Dubai, UAE, offering world-class facilities and business-friendly services to entrepreneurs, startups, and established companies. Positioned strategically next to Al Maktoum International Airport and Jebel Ali Port, DSFZ is ideal for companies involved in logistics, e-commerce, aviation, trade, and more. The zone provides a comprehensive range of services to ensure a smooth and efficient company formation process.

Steps to Form a Company in Dubai South Free Zone (DSFZ)

1

Choose a Business Activity:

  • DSFZ supports various business activities including trading, logistics, aviation, e-commerce, media, consultancy, and more. Choose an activity that best suits your business needs.
    • 2

    Select a Legal Structure:

  • Free Zone Establishment (FZE): For a company with a single shareholder.
  • Free Zone Company (FZC): For companies with multiple shareholders.
  • Branch Office: For foreign or local companies wishing to establish a branch in Dubai South.
    • 3

    Reserve a Trade Name:

  • Select a unique trade name for your company in line with DSFZ naming guidelines.
    • 4

    Submit Application Form:

  • Complete the application form and submit all required documents, such as business activity, shareholders, and legal structure.
    • 5

    Prepare Required Documentation:

  • Passport copies of shareholders, directors, and managers.
  • Passport-sized photographs.
  • Proof of address.
  • No Objection Certificate (NOC) for UAE residents (if applicable).
    • 6

    Choose Office Space:

  • Dubai South offers a variety of office options including flexi-desks, serviced offices, and warehouses depending on the business needs.
    • 7

    Pay Fees:

  • Pay the registration and licensing fees as per the business structure and office space selected.
    • 8

    Obtain the Business License:

  • Once all documentation is verified and the fees are paid, DSFZ will issue your business license and certificate of incorporation.
    • 9

    Open a Corporate Bank Account:

  • Open a corporate bank account using your company’s documents.
    • 10

    Apply for Visas:

  • Based on the office space and the nature of the business, apply for visas for investors, employees, and dependents.
